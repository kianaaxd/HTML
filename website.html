Counter-Strike 2

Overview

It is a sequel to the Counter-Strike: Global Offensive. It was revealed on March 22, 2023, and it was launched on September 27, 2023, taking the place of Global Offensive on Steam.

Gameplay

The majority of the gameplay of CS2 is still quite similar to that of its predecessor, CS:GO, if not identical. However, the game has undergone a few small to major adjustments in order to make the most of the Source 2 Engine.

Game Modes

There are several multiplayer game types in Counter-Strike 2. Classic Competitive and Classic Casual: Traditional game modes for hostage rescue and bomb defusal. To better accommodate casual and competitive players, the regulations for each are different: casual and competitive. Bomb Scenario: The Counter-Terrorists must guard the bombsite while the Terrorists must place C4 there. Wingman: two vs two games on compact bomb defusal terrain. Premier Competitive: A variant of Competitive in which participants use a veto mechanism to choose which Active Duty maps to play.

Maps

While the game has several maps, the ones that are currently in use are Mirage, Nuke, Overpass, Vertigo, Ancient, Inferno, and Anubis.

  • Mirage - The Bomb Defusal map Mirage (de_mirage), originally called Strike, is available for Counter-Strike: Global Offensive and Counter-Strike 2. June 6, 2013, saw the addition of the map to the game. The map is themed like a Middle Eastern town, but because of its close resemblance to Moroccan style architecture, it is likely set somewhere in Morocco.

  • Nuke - The map takes place in a warehouse containing nuclear materials or a nuclear power plant. In Global Offensive, the original version of the map takes place a German nuclear power plant, while the revamped version takes place in an American one. Unlike most Counter-Strike bomb defusal maps, the map revolves around a central structure rather than multiple lanes, with the two bombsites being overlapped on top of each other.

  • Overpass - The map's setting is a canal bridge with a park erected above it in Berlin, Germany. The GSG-9 must guard a stopped military shipment on a canal overpass, while the Phoenix Connexion can either attack the container directly or attempt to demolish the flyover by bombing the pillar beneath. Counter-Terrorists spawn at bombsite A, where the crippled freight vehicle is parked. Meanwhile, the Terrorist Spawn Zone is located at the canal's bottom. Bombsite B is located below the CT Spawn Zone and is easily accessible via a stairway. Snipers can readily guard this area since a sniper nest is strategically placed throughout the location.

  • Vertigo - Vertigo is set in a building that is currently under construction and centers on the battle between the Counter-Terrorist and Terrorist squads. The Terrorists' main goal is to bomb the building, and the CTs must stop them. The bombsites and Counter-Terrorist spawn are both on the top level of the map, whereas the Terrorist spawn is on the lower floor near the staircase.

  • Ancient - Ancient looks to take place in an archeological site in the middle of a jungle (most likely in Central America). Across the globe, there exist numerous artifacts such as pottery, artworks, and structures that mimic those of the Aztec empire. Each bombsite is referred to as a 'plaza' on the placard and has a separate theme color. Bombsite A is orange with tarps, paint, and signage, whereas Bombsite B is pastel blue.

  • Inferno - The map depicts a tiny village with European architecture. The existence of the Separatist group in the Global Offensive edition implies that the map is located in Italy, as indicated by the Italian-language signage spotted across the area. However, the separatist party is also present in Spain's Basque territory and in France, so it is possible that it will be present there as well. The map is generally rectangular, with the Counter-Terrorists spawning in one corner, the Terrorists in the diagonally opposite corner, and the two bombsites in the remaining two corners.

  • Anubis - Anubis (de_anubis) is a Bomb Defusal map made by Roald, jakuza, and jd40 that appears in Counter-Strike: Global Offensive. It was introduced to the game on March 31, 2020, with Chlorine. Anubis was initially released in Scrimmage mode alone, but it later made its way into the Active Duty Group, replacing Dust II. Anubis was promoted from Scrimmage to Competitive on April 10, 2020, after just ten days in the game. It was the second map to be switched to Competitive since the introduction of Scrimmage, the first being the new Cache. Anubis was deleted from the game on May 3, 2021, but was re-added on August 16, 2022, to commemorate Counter-Strike: Global Offensive's tenth anniversary. Following the IEM Rio 2022 Major, it was added to the Active Duty Group in the November 18, 2022 update. This is the second community-created map to make it into the Active Duty Group, following Cache.
